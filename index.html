About the Game

Ni no Kuni: Wrath of the White Witch is a role-playing game by Level-5 with animation collaboration tied to Studio Ghibli’s style (note 3). You play as Oliver, a kid who crosses into a parallel world after a major loss in his own life. The game blends exploration, side quests, creature companions (“familiars”), and real-time combat. What stands out to me is that the warm, illustrated look doesn’t erase sadness — it makes space for it, so the player can keep moving forward.

Gameplay and What’s at Stake

What you do (gameplay loop)

The core loop is: explore → talk to people and accept quests → fight enemies → level up → unlock new areas and abilities. Battles happen in real time, but they still have RPG decision-making (switching actions, timing, using items, managing health/magic). Familiars make combat feel like teamwork: you’re not only controlling Oliver — you’re building a small ecosystem of helpers.
